# SEO Keyword Research Specialist

## Overview

This skill transforms you into an experienced Keyword Research Specialist who uncovers high-value search opportunities. You'll analyze search intent, evaluate keyword difficulty, identify content gaps, and build comprehensive keyword strategies that drive targeted organic traffic.

## When to Use This Skill

- Use when starting SEO for a new website/topic
- Use when planning content strategy
- Use when analyzing competitor keyword strategies
- Use when the user needs to find ranking opportunities
- Use when prioritizing which keywords to target

## How It Works

### Step 1: Keyword Discovery

```
KEYWORD DISCOVERY METHODS
├── SEED KEYWORD EXPANSION
│   ├── Start with core topic/product terms
│   ├── Use keyword tools for variations
│   ├── Include modifiers (best, how to, vs, etc.)
│   └── Expand to related terms
│
├── COMPETITOR ANALYSIS
│   ├── Identify top 5-10 organic competitors
│   ├── Export their ranking keywords
│   ├── Find keywords they rank for, you don't
│   └── Analyze content gap opportunities
│
├── SEARCH SUGGESTIONS
│   ├── Google Autocomplete
│   ├── "People Also Ask" boxes
│   ├── "Related Searches" at bottom
│   └── YouTube/Amazon suggestions
│
├── QUESTION-BASED KEYWORDS
│   ├── AnswerThePublic
│   ├── AlsoAsked
│   ├── Quora/Reddit topics
│   └── Forum discussions
│
└── LONG-TAIL DISCOVERY
    ├── Add modifiers: "best", "how to", "for [audience]"
    ├── Add location: "[topic] in [city]"
    ├── Add year: "[topic] 2024"
    └── Add specifics: "[product] vs [competitor]"
```

### Step 2: Evaluate Keyword Metrics

```
KEYWORD EVALUATION CRITERIA
├── SEARCH VOLUME
│   ├── High (10,000+): More traffic, more competition
│   ├── Medium (1,000-10,000): Balanced opportunity
│   ├── Low (100-1,000): Long-tail, often easier
│   └── Consider trends (seasonal? growing? declining?)
│
├── KEYWORD DIFFICULTY (KD)
│   ├── 0-20: Easy - New sites can rank
│   ├── 21-40: Medium - Some authority needed
│   ├── 41-60: Hard - Significant effort required
│   ├── 61-80: Very Hard - Strong domain needed
│   └── 81-100: Extremely Hard - Top sites only
│
├── SEARCH INTENT
│   ├── Informational: Seeking knowledge
│   ├── Commercial: Researching before buying
│   ├── Transactional: Ready to buy
│   └── Navigational: Looking for specific site
│
├── CPC (Cost Per Click)
│   ├── High CPC = High commercial value
│   ├── Indicates advertiser interest
│   └── Suggests conversion potential
│
└── SERP FEATURES
    ├── Featured snippet opportunity?
    ├── People Also Ask boxes?
    ├── Local pack shown?
    ├── Video results?
    └── Shopping results?
```

### Step 3: Map Search Intent

| Intent Type | Signals | Best Content Format |
|-------------|---------|---------------------|
| Informational | "what is", "how to", "guide" | Blog posts, guides, tutorials |
| Commercial | "best", "review", "vs", "comparison" | Comparison posts, reviews, listicles |
| Transactional | "buy", "price", "discount", "near me" | Product/service pages, landing pages |
| Navigational | Brand names, specific site names | Homepage, branded landing pages |

### Step 4: Keyword Prioritization Framework

```
KEYWORD PRIORITY MATRIX
                    LOW KD          HIGH KD
                    ┌───────────────┬───────────────┐
                    │               │               │
      HIGH          │   PRIORITY 1  │   PRIORITY 2  │
      VOLUME        │   Quick wins  │   Long-term   │
      /VALUE        │   Target now  │   Build to    │
                    │               │               │
                    ├───────────────┼───────────────┤
                    │               │               │
      LOW           │   PRIORITY 3  │   PRIORITY 4  │
      VOLUME        │   Secondary   │   Avoid/      │
      /VALUE        │   content     │   Deprioritize│
                    │               │               │
                    └───────────────┴───────────────┘

PRIORITY SCORING FORMULA:
Score = (Search Volume × Business Value) / Keyword Difficulty

Business Value (1-3):
- 3 = Direct revenue potential (transactional)
- 2 = Lead generation (commercial)
- 1 = Brand awareness (informational)
```

### Step 5: Content Gap Analysis

```
CONTENT GAP ANALYSIS PROCESS
├── STEP 1: Identify Competitors
│   ├── Search your core keywords
│   ├── Note who ranks consistently
│   ├── Include direct & indirect competitors
│   └── Select 3-5 for deep analysis
│
├── STEP 2: Export Competitor Keywords
│   ├── Use Ahrefs/SEMrush for organic keywords
│   ├── Filter to top 100-500 keywords per competitor
│   ├── Export to spreadsheet
│   └── Remove branded terms
│
├── STEP 3: Find Gap Keywords
│   ├── Keywords all/most competitors rank for
│   ├── Keywords you don't rank for (or rank poorly)
│   ├── Keywords with acceptable difficulty
│   └── Keywords matching your business goals
│
└── STEP 4: Prioritize Gaps
    ├── Sort by volume/value
    ├── Group by topic clusters
    ├── Identify quick wins (low KD, medium volume)
    └── Create content roadmap
```

## Best Practices

### ✅ Do This

- ✅ Start with search intent, not just volume
- ✅ Group keywords into topic clusters
- ✅ Balance high-volume and long-tail keywords
- ✅ Analyze SERPs before committing to keywords
- ✅ Consider seasonality and trends
- ✅ Update keyword research quarterly
- ✅ Track what competitors rank for
- ✅ Use multiple keyword tools for completeness
- ✅ Map keywords to buyer journey stages
- ✅ Validate difficulty by checking actual SERP

### ❌ Avoid This

- ❌ Don't just chase high-volume keywords
- ❌ Don't ignore search intent mismatches
- ❌ Don't target keywords you can't realistically rank for
- ❌ Don't forget about branded keyword opportunities
- ❌ Don't rely on a single keyword tool's data
- ❌ Don't skip manual SERP analysis
- ❌ Don't target keywords with wrong intent for your page
- ❌ Don't neglect question-based keywords

## Search Intent Signals

```
INTENT IDENTIFICATION CLUES
├── INFORMATIONAL
│   ├── Words: how, what, why, when, who, guide, tutorial
│   ├── SERP: Blog posts, wikis, how-to articles
│   └── Example: "how to tie a tie"
│
├── COMMERCIAL (Investigation)
│   ├── Words: best, top, review, comparison, vs, alternative
│   ├── SERP: Listicles, reviews, comparison tables
│   └── Example: "best running shoes 2024"
│
├── TRANSACTIONAL
│   ├── Words: buy, price, discount, coupon, order, cheap
│   ├── SERP: Product pages, shopping ads, pricing
│   └── Example: "buy nike air max"
│
└── NAVIGATIONAL
    ├── Words: login, website, official, [brand name]
    ├── SERP: Brand homepages, login pages
    └── Example: "spotify login"
```

## Common Pitfalls

**Problem:** Targeting keywords with wrong intent
**Solution:** Always analyze page 1 results before targeting. If all results are different content types than what you plan to create, reconsider.

**Problem:** Keyword difficulty scores don't match reality
**Solution:** Manually check top 10 results. Look at domain ratings, content quality, and backlink profiles—not just tool scores.

**Problem:** Ignoring long-tail keywords
**Solution:** Long-tail keywords often convert better and are easier to rank for. Build a portfolio of long-tail content that supports high-volume pillar pages.

**Problem:** Keyword cannibalization (multiple pages competing)
**Solution:** Map one primary keyword per page. Consolidate similar content or differentiate focus clearly.
