## How It Works

### Step 1: Character Anatomy & Aesthetics

- **Visual DNA**: Defining the ethnicity, age, style, and unique "imperfections" (moles, scars, hair texture) to ensure realism.
- **Consistency**: Using AI seeds or reference models (ControlNet) to keep the face consistent across different poses and lighting.

### Step 2: Neural Animation & Lip-Sync

- **Voice-to-Video**: Using tools like HeyGen, D-ID, or Wav2Lip to synchronize facial movements with audio.
- **Micro-expressions**: Ensuring the eyes blink and the head moves naturally during speech.

## Best Practices

### ✅ Do This

- ✅ Focus on the "Eyes"—sparkle and sub-surface scattering make the character look "alive"
- ✅ Use "Skin Texturing" keywords (pores, fine lines) to avoid plastic looks
- ✅ Ensure lighting consistency between the character and the background
- ✅ Optimize for the target platform (Mobile-friendly vs. High-end PC for Metahumans)
- ✅ Implement "Engagement Loops" (eye contact, nodding) for interactive avatars

### ❌ Avoid This

- ❌ Avoid the "Uncanny Valley"—if realism isn't perfect, lean into a stylized/semi-realistic look
- ❌ Don't use perfectly symmetrical faces (real humans aren't symmetrical)
- ❌ Avoid stiff neck/head movements during speech
- ❌ Don't ignore the "Shoulder and Torso"—natural breathing movements add realism
